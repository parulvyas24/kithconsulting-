Data Requirements

What we need from you — upfront.

We'll send each portco a structured checklist organized by GHG Protocol scope and emission category. Their teams mark what's material and what data exists. That tells us where to focus and where the gaps are.

Scope 1 — Direct Emissions
Sources owned or controlled
■ Stationary combustion (boilers, furnaces, generators) ■ Mobile combustion (fleet vehicles, forklifts) ■ Process emissions (manufacturing, chemical) ■ Fugitive emissions (refrigerants, HVAC leaks)
High materiality: Sterling, Clearview
Scope 2 — Indirect (Energy)
Purchased energy
■ Purchased electricity (by facility, 12–24 mo.) ■ Purchased steam / heating ■ Purchased cooling ■ Grid emission factors by location
Material for all portcos — universal baseline
Scope 3 — Value Chain
Upstream & downstream
■ Purchased goods & services (spend data) ■ Fuel & energy-related activities ■ Transportation & distribution ■ Waste generated in operations ■ Business travel & employee commuting ■ Use of sold products (if applicable)
Materiality varies by sector — checklist filters by company
🌎 From Northwood (Fund-Level)
■ LP reporting templates & ESG questionnaire history ■ IC memo templates & DD checklists ■ Portfolio financials (revenue, EBITDA by portco) ■ Existing ESG policies, prior assessments or consultant work
🏭 From Each Portco (Operational)
■ Facility list with square footage & headcount ■ Org chart (who owns EHS, facilities, sustainability) ■ Any existing environmental permits or compliance filings ■ Safety, DEI, workforce, or environmental reports (if any)
The attached Excel is the real checklist. Each portco gets a tab. Every GHG Protocol category listed. They mark: Material? Yes/No. Data available? Yes/No/Partial. That tells us exactly where to dig and where the gaps are before we write a single formula.
