Key Insight: Defense Revenue at Risk

Sterling's three largest customers — Boeing, Raytheon, and L3Harris — have all included carbon intensity requirements in recent RFPs. Defense revenue is $5.4M/month (48% of Sterling's revenue). Failing to establish emissions baselines and reduction targets by 2027 puts this revenue at risk.

The CNC automation investment ($10.5M) will improve energy efficiency, but Sterling needs a comprehensive emissions strategy to satisfy defense customer requirements. Mandatory supplier emissions reporting is expected by 2027.
