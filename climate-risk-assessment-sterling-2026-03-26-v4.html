Financial metrics sourced from portfolio/_portfolio-dashboard.md and sterling-precision-manufacturing/portco-status.md. Customer ESG requirements sourced from Sterling climate questionnaire (December 2025, submitted by COO Derek Williams). Scenario analysis carbon price trajectories from NGFS Phase IV (2023) median estimates. Physical risk projections from NOAA Climate Explorer for Northeast Ohio. IEA World Energy Outlook 2023 and IPCC AR6 Working Group III cross-referenced for scenario framing.

2. Structural Consistency

Scope 1 (2,074 mt) + Scope 2 (3,970 mt) = 6,044 mt total, consistent across all sections including MACC curve, scenario analysis carbon cost calculations, and TCFD preview. Revenue ($168M), EBITDA ($30.2M), headcount (420), and facility count (3) match portfolio dashboard values. Emissions intensity (36.0 mt/$M) = 6,044 / 168. MACC curve total addressable abatement (1,000–2,500 mt) is consistent with sum of individual initiative ranges for Scope 1+2 measures. Scenario analysis carbon costs calculated as stated price × 6,044 mt, verified across all three scenarios. Section numbers updated to reflect insertion of Section 4 (Climate Scenario Analysis). Deliverable gap table references match section numbers throughout.

3. Adversarial Review

Scope 3 estimates carry significant uncertainty (2–5x range reflects genuine methodological uncertainty for metal fabrication without procurement data). MACC curve accuracy explicitly flagged as ±30–50% from public reference data; company-specific data would improve to ±10–15%. R-22 refrigerant emissions are understated if a catastrophic leak occurs (50 lbs R-22 = ~88 mt CO2e if fully released). TCFD governance and strategy pillars identified as having no public data fallback. Customer revenue-at-risk figure ($25–42M) is Sterling management's estimate, not independently verified. EIA Ohio industrial rates used as proxy; Sterling's actual tariff may differ. Scenario analysis financial impact estimates are directional (±30–40%) and based on Sterling's current cost structure; not audited financial projections. Disorderly scenario revenue-at-risk ($8–17M) assumes 25–40% of defense revenue ($63.8M) is at risk if unprepared, consistent with management's estimate of programs requiring ESG compliance.

4. Assumption Flagging

Hard data: Scope 1+2 emissions (from utility bills via carbon CSV), financial metrics (from Northwood portfolio data), facility locations and characteristics. Public reference data: EIA Ohio industrial electricity ($0.08/kWh) and gas ($0.65/therm) rates, DOE efficiency benchmarks, EPA emission factors, NOAA/FEMA physical risk projections, RS Means cost estimates, NREL PVWatts solar output, NGFS Phase IV carbon price trajectories, IEA WEO 2023 scenario parameters, IPCC AR6 SSP pathway definitions. Management guidance: Energy costs ($1.8M), R-22 replacement cost range, customer ESG requirement timelines, revenue at risk. Firm/consultant estimate: Scope 3 range (EPA benchmarks), MACC savings calculations, physical risk trajectory, scenario analysis financial impact ranges (±30–40% directional estimates based on Sterling's current cost structure). All Scope 3 figures, MACC economics, and scenario financial impacts are explicitly flagged as estimates, not measured or audited values.
