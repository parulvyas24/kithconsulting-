Key Findings

Top 11 findings ranked by materiality. Financial data findings included alongside climate gaps.

  1. Fund II drawn capital discrepancy: $149M. Portfolio dashboard reports $754M drawn (69%). Firm overview reports $825M drawn (75%). Both cite the same date (Jan 31, 2026). Remaining capacity diverges accordingly: $346M vs. $275M. Needs reconciliation before LP letter. CRITICAL
  2. Scope 3 emissions data is absent across the entire portfolio. Zero companies have any value chain emissions data. For Clearview (environmental services) and Harvest Ridge (food supply chain), Scope 3 likely represents 80%+ of total emissions footprint. CRITICAL
  3. MACC curve input data completely absent. No utility rate schedules, equipment specifications, or vendor quotes exist at any portfolio company. Public benchmarks (EPA, DOE, RS Means) provide ±30-50% accuracy vs. ±10-15% with actual data. CRITICAL
  4. 4 ESG Supabase tables have zero rows. esg_portco_scores, esg_portco_composites, esg_climate_risk, and esg_climate_composites are defined but empty. The schema exists; the data pipeline to populate them does not. HIGH
  5. Budget data incomplete for 3 portcos. Keystone, Pinnacle, and Ridgeline lack budget data in Supabase portco_financials. All three are in 100-day or early integration. Budget vs. actual variance analysis is not possible for these companies. HIGH
  6. Patriot Staffing has worst climate data quality: 4% Grade A facilities. Patriot is also below plan on financial performance. Climate data collection is not on management's agenda while margin recovery is the priority. HIGH
  7. 40% of facility energy data is estimated, not metered. Across 47 facilities, approximately 19 rely on spend-based proxies or square-footage estimates rather than utility bill data. This makes any future emissions calculations unreliable without data remediation. HIGH
  8. No emissions baselines computed for any portco. Raw energy data exists but has not been converted to tCO2e using GHG Protocol emission factors. The calculation is straightforward once data quality is confirmed. MEDIUM
  9. Meridian exit process may face ESG DD gap. Exit prep underway with sell-side advisor selection in Q1 2026. Buyers increasingly request climate data in DD. No emissions baseline, no TCFD alignment, no reduction targets. MEDIUM
  10. Fund II EBITDA aggregates show minor discrepancy. Dashboard shows Fund II EBITDA $139.5M. Firm overview shows $135.8M. Difference of $3.7M. Likely driven by different reporting dates or Harvest Ridge EBITDA ($22.8M vs. $22.0M). MEDIUM
  11. No climate governance structures at any portco. No board-level climate oversight, no management accountability for emissions, no climate risk in enterprise risk frameworks. Governance is a prerequisite for framework alignment. MEDIUM

8.4 — Key Assumptions & Limitations
  • All Scope 2 uses location-based method (eGRID regional factors). Market-based method not applied (no RECs or PPAs reported).
  • December data for Sterling and Harvest Ridge is annualized from 11 months — actual Dec bills not yet available.
  • Scope 3 estimates (Sterling only) use EPA EEIO spend-based factors and World Steel Association production factors as cross-check. Range reflects methodology uncertainty.
  • Facilities reporting 0 kWh (NovaCare) excluded from totals rather than assumed zero.
  • Patriot’s Evansville and Rockford facilities excluded (no verifiable data).
  • Refrigerant emissions based on estimated annual leak rate (5–10% of charge), not measured losses.
  • No Scope 3 estimates attempted for companies other than Sterling due to insufficient sector benchmarking data at this stage.
